Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our DevOps & CI/CD services.

What is the difference between CI and CD?

Continuous Integration (CI) is the practice of automatically building and testing code changes when they're committed to a shared repository. Continuous Delivery (CD) extends this by automatically deploying code changes to staging environments and preparing them for production. Continuous Deployment goes a step further by automatically deploying changes to production after passing all tests.

How long does it take to implement DevOps practices?

Implementing DevOps is a journey rather than a destination. Initial pipeline setup and automation can be achieved in a few weeks, but the full cultural and process transformation typically takes 6-12 months. We recommend a phased approach, starting with high-impact areas and gradually expanding across your organization.

Which CI/CD tools do you recommend?

We work with a variety of CI/CD tools including Jenkins, GitLab CI, GitHub Actions, CircleCI, and Azure DevOps. Our recommendation depends on your existing technology stack, team expertise, and specific requirements. We focus on selecting tools that integrate well with your ecosystem and meet your scalability needs.

How do you ensure security in automated pipelines?

We implement DevSecOps practices by integrating security testing throughout the pipeline, including SAST, DAST, dependency scanning, and container security. We also implement secure infrastructure practices, least privilege access controls, and secrets management to protect your environments and applications.

Can DevOps practices work with legacy applications?

Yes, DevOps practices can be adapted for legacy applications, though the approach may differ from greenfield projects. We focus on incremental improvements, starting with version control, automated testing, and deployment automation where possible, while respecting the constraints of legacy systems.
